Flash-Flood Risk for Jizan — 2025-08-23

Probability: 12.53% (low risk) - Model verified ROC-AUC: 0.873 (highly reliable model)

Why this risk exists

The physical mechanisms driving flash-flood risk in Jizan are:

  • Active Red Sea Trough (ARST) — A low-pressure trough over the Red Sea that draws in moist air. When it interacts with eastward-moving Mediterranean cyclones and the Arabian Anticyclone, it can produce intense rainfall. *(de Vries et al., 2013)*

  • Moisture Transport — Surface fluxes over the Red Sea supply low-level moist air, contributing ~30–40% of regional rainfall. Stronger winds can enhance latent heat fluxes, fueling convection. *(ResearchGate case study; MDPI Atmosphere, 2019)*

  • Orographic Lift — The Hejaz/Asir mountains force moist air upward, enhancing rainfall on the windward side (where Jizan is located).

    • Recommendation

    The risk on August 23 is low (~12.5%), so no major concern. However, Jizan is climatologically prone to flash floods during the summer monsoon season (July–September) due to the Red Sea Trough and orographic effects. Keep an eye on short-term forecasts if you're planning activities in wadi (valley) areas.
