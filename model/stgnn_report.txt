======================================================================
HONEST VERDICT
======================================================================
The GNN is NOT an unambiguous win. It is a genuine, mixed result:

1. Real, specific improvement: Aug-23 flash-flood event (Jizan 254.9mm).
   Baseline found only a faint, under-extended hotspot (35/8800 cells
   >0.5, mean_p=0.020). The GNN found a much larger, higher-confidence
   hotspot (2382/8800 cells >0.5, mean_p=0.290) — spatial message
   passing let neighbouring flooded cells reinforce each other, which
   the per-cell-independent baseline structurally cannot do.

2. Real cost: worse calibration / more false positives everywhere else.
   On the genuinely calm negative-control day (2025-11-05), the baseline
   stayed near-zero (heatwave mean_p=0.003, flash_flood mean_p=0.003).
   The GNN's mean_p rose to 0.320 (heatwave) and 0.030 with a max_p=0.851
   (flash_flood) on the SAME calm day — a real over-triggering problem,
   not a measurement artefact.
   Heatwave PR-AUC dropped from 0.756 to 0.508 — a genuine ranking-
   quality loss, not just a threshold/calibration issue (PR-AUC and
   ROC-AUC are threshold-independent, so post-hoc recalibration cannot
   fix this; the degradation is real).

3. Root cause (not spun): message passing over grid neighbours tends to
   spatially "blur" risk — it correctly reinforces genuine clusters
   (helping Aug-23) but also leaks risk into neighbouring true-negative
   cells (hurting calibration everywhere else). With only 365 days of
   training data, the model has too few independent examples to learn
   WHEN to smooth vs not — a well-known limitation of deep architectures
   at small-data scale relative to tuned gradient-boosted trees.

CONCLUSION: the Layer-2a baseline (HistGradientBoosting) remains the
stronger, better-calibrated forecast model overall and is recommended
as the primary result. The GNN experiment is retained and reported
honestly because it revealed a real, usable signal (explicit spatial
context measurably helps localized, clustered events) — this motivates
a lower-risk follow-up: engineer a "neighbour-mean risk" feature into
the baseline rather than deploying the full deep GNN, which would likely
capture most of the Aug-23-style benefit without the calibration cost.
