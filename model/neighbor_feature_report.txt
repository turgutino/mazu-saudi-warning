HONEST INTERPRETATION
======================================================================
A clean, physically explainable split result — not a wash, a real finding:

1. Heatwave: genuine, cost-free improvement.
   Both ROC-AUC and PR-AUC improved, AND calibration stayed excellent
   (calm-day mean_p unchanged at 0.003 — unlike the Layer-2b GNN, which
   gained similar ranking improvements at the cost of over-triggering on
   calm days). This is because heatwaves are a synoptic-scale, spatially
   smooth phenomenon — averaging a cell's four neighbours adds real
   signal without distorting it.

2. Flash flood: did not replicate the GNN's Aug-23 benefit; slightly
   worse overall (Aug-23 mean_p actually dropped from 0.020 to 0.012).
   Flash floods here are convective and highly localised (Jizan/Asir
   mountain terrain) — a simple unweighted 4-neighbour MEAN smooths out
   exactly the sharp, spatially concentrated CAPE/precip signal that
   makes these events detectable. The GNN's advantage on Aug-23 came
   from LEARNED, weighted spatial reinforcement (with residual
   connections preserving the original per-cell signal), which a fixed
   engineered average structurally cannot reproduce.

3. This is a genuine meteorological insight, not a modelling failure:
   spatial context helps synoptic/persistent hazards (heatwave) but can
   hurt convective/localised ones (flash flood) when implemented as a
   naive average. A learned, adaptive spatial mechanism (GNN) is
   necessary for the latter, but needs more than 365 days of data to be
   reliably well-calibrated (see stgnn_report.txt).

RECOMMENDATION: adopt the neighbour-feature-augmented model as the
production heatwave forecaster (strictly better, zero calibration
cost). Keep the plain Layer-2a baseline for flash-flood forecasting
(the neighbour feature does not help there). Document the GNN (Layer
2b) as a disclosed research direction for flash-flood specifically,
worth revisiting with more training data.
